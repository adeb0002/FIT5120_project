Personalised Recommendations

Dishwasher Usage:

Consider running your dishwasher only when it’s full and using the eco or energy-saving mode. Reducing the frequency of washes and opting for cooler water settings can significantly cut energy consumption. By doing so, you not only lower your carbon footprint but also save on electricity bills. Each cycle you avoid saves approximately 1 kWh, which can add up to noticeable savings over time.

Air Conditioner Usage:

Set your air conditioner to a higher temperature in summer (around 24-26°C) and lower in winter (around 18-20°C). These small adjustments can reduce energy use by up to 10%, leading to lower utility bills and reduced greenhouse gas emissions.

Refrigerator Usage:

Ensure your refrigerator is set between 3°C and 5°C and keep it well-stocked, but not overfilled. Regularly defrosting and cleaning the coils can improve efficiency. By maintaining optimal settings and reducing energy leakage, you can save energy and extend the life of your appliance, reducing both your carbon footprint and energy expenses.

Washing Machine Usage:

Wash your clothes in cold water whenever possible and wait for a full load before running the machine. Skipping the dryer and air-drying clothes can further reduce your energy usage. Cold washing can reduce energy use by up to 80% per wash, leading to significant savings on your energy bill while lowering CO2 emissions.

Public Transport Usage:

Whenever feasible, opt for public transport over driving. Not only does this reduce your personal carbon emissions, but it also saves money on fuel, parking, and vehicle maintenance. Regular use of public transport can cut your transportation costs by up to 50% while significantly decreasing your carbon footprint.
