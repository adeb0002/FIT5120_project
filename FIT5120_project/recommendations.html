Recommendations

Explore tailored recommendations that help reduce your carbon emissions or save costs. Choose between emissions-focused or cost-saving tips to optimise your daily habits and contribute to a more sustainable future.

Dishwasher Usage:

Run your dishwasher only when it's full and use the energy-saving mode. Reducing the frequency of washes and opting for cooler water settings can lower energy consumption, reducing your carbon footprint.

Air Conditioner Usage:

Set your air conditioner to a higher temperature in summer and lower in winter to reduce energy usage. This small adjustment can decrease greenhouse gas emissions.

Refrigerator Efficiency:

Keep your refrigerator between 3°C and 5°C, and defrost it regularly to improve energy efficiency. Doing so reduces emissions and prolongs the appliance's life.

Public Transport Usage:

Use public transport instead of driving when possible. This helps reduce your transportation emissions and promotes cleaner air quality in your city.

Maximise Solar Energy:

If you have solar panels, use high-energy appliances like washing machines and dishwashers during the day to reduce reliance on the grid and save money on electricity bills.

Cold Water for Laundry:

Washing clothes in cold water can reduce energy use by up to 80% per wash. This simple switch can lead to significant savings on your energy bills while still getting your clothes clean.

Air-Dry Clothes:

Skipping the dryer and air-drying clothes can save energy and lower your electricity costs. This eco-friendly habit is especially effective during warmer months.

Government Rebates:

Take advantage of government rebates to offset costs. Explore the following programs:

  • Victorian Energy Upgrades
  • Solar Panel Rebate
  • Energy Bill Relief Fund
