Climate Change 101: Introduction to Greenhouse Gases

The Earth’s atmosphere is composed of various gases, including greenhouse gases. These gases are necessary to keep the Earth warm. However, excessive concentrations of greenhouse gases can lead to dangerous warming.

The four main types of greenhouse gases are:

  • Carbon Dioxide (CO2)
  • Methane (CH4)
  • Nitrous Oxide (N2O)
  • Fluorinated Gases

CO2 (carbon dioxide) is the most used greenhouse gas (GHG) for metrics because:

  • CO2 is the most abundant greenhouse gas released by human activities.
  • CO2 remains in the atmosphere for hundreds to thousands of years, making its impact long-lasting compared to other greenhouse gases.
  • CO2 emissions are relatively easy to quantify and track.

Global temperatures have increased as the concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere has risen. This is the most dangerous outcome of climate change.

Why Do Changing Temperatures Matter?

A warmer climate changes weather patterns, making wet areas wetter and dry areas drier. This could lead to extensive flooding in some regions and water shortages in others. Leading economies have committed to reaching net-zero emissions by 2050 to keep global warming to 1.5°C above pre-industrial temperatures. While even 1.5°C of warming could lead to harmful global consequences, the implications of 2°C of warming are even worse:

  • More than a third of the global population could experience severe heatwaves at least every 5 years.
  • Widespread food shortages could arise in Southern Africa, the Mediterranean, Central Europe, and the Amazon.
  • Over 70% of Earth’s coastlines could see sea levels rise by 50 cm, resulting in extensive coastal flooding.
  • More than 40% of the permafrost in the Arctic could disappear.

How Do My Daily Actions Affect Climate Change?

Here's how this chain of events works where your daily actions become greenhouse gas emissions:

  1. Everyday activities rely on energy from burning fossil fuels, which release GHGs.
  2. These GHGs accumulate in the atmosphere, trapping heat that would otherwise escape into space.
  3. As more GHGs are emitted, the Earth's average temperature rises.
  4. Rising temperatures disrupt natural systems, altering weather patterns and increasing the frequency of extreme events.
  5. These changes result in harsher climate conditions, devastating agriculture, biodiversity, and human health globally.

How to Prevent Further Warming

There are two ways to prevent further warming:

  • Reflect more sunlight back into space (with harmful side effects).
  • Reduce greenhouse gas concentrations in the atmosphere (the safer option).

However, reflecting sunlight back into space poses harmful potential implications for global weather, so initiatives like ours around the world focus on reducing greenhouse gas emissions instead.

How Do We Decarbonise?

Decarbonising personal activities is key to reducing our carbon footprint and combating climate change. Whether it's through smarter transportation choices, home energy efficiency, or sustainable shopping habits, there are numerous ways to make a positive impact.
